Kabyle uses the **Berber Latin alphabet** (*Agemmay Alatin n Teqbaylit*), a standardized script heavily optimized for Tamazight phonology. It features 34 native letters (24 standard international Latin units and 10 specialized characters).

Key features:

* **Diacritics for Emphasis:** A dot below (**ḍ, ṛ, ṣ, ṭ, ẓ**) indicates pharyngealization (emphatic consonants).
* **Affricates:** The caron accent is used exclusively on **č** (/t͡ʃ/) and **ǧ** (/d͡ʒ/). *(Note: The cedilla "ç" is not part of the standard Kabyle orthography neither is the apostrophe)*.
* **Greek Adaptations:** The characters **ɣ** (Latin small letter gamma) and **ɛ** (Latin small letter open e) are utilized to map the voiced velar fricative and voiced pharyngeal fricative without using confusing multi-letter digraphs.
* **Spirantization:** Most simple stop consonants (*b, d, g, k, t*) have a spirantized (fricative) pronunciation contextual to their environment, but they retain their single-letter baseline spellings in standard orthography.

The writing system is highly morpho-phonemic, establishing an incredibly tight structural mapping between text representations and oral execution.
