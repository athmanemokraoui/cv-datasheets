The main variants of Kabyle include:

* **Western / Central Kabyle** -- Spoken in Greater Kabylie (e.g., Tizi Ouzou, Larbaa n Ath Irathen, At Yanni, Bouira). Historically, it has served as the baseline for major modern lexicographical works.
* **Eastern Kabyle** -- Spoken in Lesser Kabylie and the Soummam Valley (e.g., Bejaia, Sidi Aich, Chemini, Ouzellaguen, Ighil Ali). It features prominent phonetic transitions such as the *ggw* mutation (e.g., *taggwurt* instead of *tabburt*).
* **Extreme-Eastern Kabyle** -- Spoken on the eastern littoral border (e.g., Aokas, Melbou, At Smaïl) moving toward the Chaoui domain.
* **Extreme-Western Kabyle** -- Spoken on the western borders of Kabylie (e.g., Tizi-Ghennif, Boghni, Draâ El Mizan), showing early contact lines with Algerian Arabic phonology.

These differences are primarily phonological and lexical; mutual intelligibility is exceptionally high across all regions. The Common Voice dataset does not currently distinguish between these variants at the recording level.
