The initial Kabyle text corpus was built primarily from Kabyle Wikipedia articles and the Tatoeba project sentence repository, integrated starting in version v17.0.

Beginning with v20.0, community-submitted sentences through the official Common Voice Sentence Collector became a primary growth engine for the dataset. These additions heavily diversified the corpus into everyday conversational topics, local cultural idioms, and general modern vocabulary.

In subsequent dataset releases, original sentence blocks crafted by Kabyle language volunteers and educators were appended. Every batch undergoes strict crowd-sourced or expert review to verify grammatical accuracy, dialectal inclusion, and cultural appropriateness before inclusion in the final text inventory.
